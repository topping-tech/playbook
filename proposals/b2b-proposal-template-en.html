1 How to Use This Proposal
This template should be completed after the first meeting and based on real customer information. The goal is not to create a long, complex document; the goal is to help the customer quickly understand in the second meeting what Topping learned, what solution is recommended, how pricing or pricing framework works, and how the pilot starts.
Best timing: after Meeting 1 / Discovery and for Meeting 2 / Proposal Presentation. A third meeting is only needed for large, multi-stakeholder, or complex accounts.

Important Note

This proposal should not be sent before collecting enough data. Without volume, route, goods type, scheduling, and repeatability details, pricing can become unrealistic or unprofitable.

4 Core Problem or Opportunity

A proposal should not only introduce a service. It should first clarify which business problem will be solved.

Hidden Costs

Too many calls, manual follow-up, lost internal team time, fragmented delivery, or unclear driver cost.

Operational Disorder

No clear schedule, no proof of delivery, no regular reporting, or delayed coordination.

End-Customer Experience

End customers do not know where orders are, when they will arrive, or how to track them.

Future Growth

If order volume increases, the current system may not scale effectively.

Suggested sentence:

Topping Courier’s goal is to move your deliveries from ad hoc and fragmented execution to a structured, trackable, and measurable process; especially for recurring, same-day, next-day, and scheduled orders.

6 Proposed Service Scope

Included

  • Pickup according to agreed schedule
  • Delivery within agreed zones
  • Same-day or next-day based on selected model
  • Recurring route option when volume is sufficient
  • Proof of delivery where required
  • Invoice and periodic reporting per agreement
  • Operational coordination through a defined channel

Excluded unless separately agreed

  • Guaranteed rush/urgent delivery
  • Very heavy goods beyond car/van capacity
  • High-risk, hazardous, illegal, or specially licensed goods
  • Unlimited driver waiting time at pickup or delivery
  • Last-minute changes beyond operational capacity
  • Custom integration or dashboard without separate scope and pricing

13 When to Suggest a Third Meeting

For simple to medium customers, this proposal should lead to a pilot decision in meeting two. For more complex opportunities, a third meeting can be justified.

Third meeting only in these cases

  • There are multiple decision-makers in the customer organization.
  • Delivery volume is high or routes are complex.
  • Pricing requires multiple separate scenarios.
  • Integration, dashboarding, custom reporting, or special workflows are required.
  • The customer requests formal internal review of the proposal.

Suggested wording to request a third meeting:

Given the volume and operational details discussed, we recommend one additional short meeting with key decision-makers to finalize the pricing scenario, operating scope, and pilot terms accurately.
