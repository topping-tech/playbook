Company Overview

What Topping Courier is building.

Who we are

Topping Courier is a growing logistics and courier company based in the Greater Toronto Area. The company is currently focused on building a practical B2B courier operation supported by technology, structured sales, customer follow-up, and repeatable operating procedures.

What we are not trying to be

At this stage, we are not trying to sell every possible delivery service to every industry. The priority is controlled growth: repeat B2B customers, manageable shipment types, predictable delivery windows, and sustainable routes.

Simple positioning: Topping Courier helps GTA businesses move small-to-medium shipments through scheduled same-day, next-day, and recurring delivery support.

Sales Mission

The first goal is not theory; it is building a real B2B pipeline.

Lead Outreach

Contact qualified businesses through phone, email, LinkedIn, and structured follow-up. The target is decision-maker conversations, not just activity volume.

Follow-up

Track every interaction, follow up consistently, and move interested prospects toward a call, meeting, trial, quote, or proposal.

Closing

Convert qualified opportunities into active customers by understanding delivery needs, matching them to our service model, and coordinating pricing approval when required.

04

Staged Role Path

We see leadership potential, but the role must start with execution first.
M1

Month 1 — Sales Execution Foundation

The first month is focused on simple, measurable sales work: lead outreach, follow-up, CRM updates, meeting booking, basic discovery, and closing support. The goal is to understand the business from the ground level and prove execution consistency.

  • Contact assigned leads and maintain follow-up discipline.
  • Learn Topping Courier’s service boundaries and ideal customer profile.
  • Join calls/meetings when needed and support closing conversations.
  • Record all activity clearly in the CRM or tracking sheet.
M2–3

Months 2–3 — Expanded Business Development

After the first month, responsibilities can expand if performance and working style are aligned. This may include lead finding, lead filtering, territory analysis, LinkedIn prospecting, basic campaign planning, and stronger ownership of the sales pipeline.

  • Research and qualify new B2B leads in priority categories.
  • Analyze lead quality by industry, location, recurring potential, and operational fit.
  • Support LinkedIn outreach and professional relationship-building.
  • Help improve scripts, objections, follow-up templates, and sales reports.
M4+

After 3–4 Months — Leadership Track Review

If there is mutual agreement and strong performance, the role may move toward an official leadership/management position in sales. This can include sales planning, pipeline ownership, reporting, training new salespeople, and eventually building a small sales team under supervision.

  • Lead sales process improvement and weekly reporting.
  • Help define territories, targets, and account priorities.
  • Participate in recruiting or supervising new sales representatives.
  • Move toward Sales Manager / Head of Sales responsibilities when the timing is right.
Leadership is possible, not automatic: The company is open to a management path, but it must be earned through execution, trust, accurate reporting, closing ability, and fit with the startup environment.

Tools, Systems & Working Style

What the sales side will use and help improve.

CRM and reporting discipline

No serious sales operation can run from memory. Every lead, contact, follow-up, meeting, objection, quote, and result must be recorded. This protects the company and the salesperson.

Merchant account and customer support

Topping Courier is building systems that allow business customers to place orders, manage delivery activity, track statuses, access invoices, and review expenses. Sales should understand these features as part of the long-term value proposition.

LinkedIn and digital prospecting

LinkedIn can be used for decision-maker discovery, professional outreach, and follow-up. It should support direct sales, not replace phone calls and real conversations.

Sales material and templates

The sales function will use short email templates, call scripts, follow-up sequences, lead qualification filters, proposal templates, and weekly performance reports.

07

Future Leadership Opportunity

Why this role can become bigger than a standard sales job.

Build the playbook

The right person can help turn early sales work into a repeatable sales process, including scripts, lead scoring, territory logic, and CRM discipline.

Own the pipeline

As the company grows, sales leadership will need to track lead sources, meetings, proposals, conversion rates, lost reasons, and customer quality.

Grow the team

Once the process is proven, the company may add new salespeople. A future sales manager would help train, supervise, and evaluate them.

Potential areas of growth: B2B account development, sales process ownership, LinkedIn/account-based prospecting, team supervision, customer relationship management, territory planning, and management reporting.
08

What Makes Someone a Strong Fit

This opportunity is not for someone who needs a fully built corporate machine.

Strong fit

  • Comfortable with startup uncertainty and direct responsibility.
  • Can do hands-on outreach, not only strategy discussions.
  • Understands B2B sales cycles and professional follow-up.
  • Can speak with owners, operations managers, and decision-makers.
  • Reports honestly, even when the result is not positive.
  • Wants to help build a sales department, not just take a title.

Weak fit

  • Only interested in a management title from day one.
  • Avoids cold outreach, follow-up, or CRM documentation.
  • Overpromises services, pricing, delivery speed, or capacity.
  • Needs a large existing team before taking action.
  • Focuses on activity volume without caring about close quality.
  • Does not adapt to a startup environment.

Decision & Next Steps
