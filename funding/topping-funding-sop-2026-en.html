Employee Mission

Primary mission: identify and pursue funding sources for Topping Courier growth, with focus on capital that can support technology development, sales, marketing, hiring, working capital, and equipment.

The employee should not only submit forms. They must build a tailored case for each organization. Some organizations want the language of "innovation and R&D," some need "market growth and cash flow," and others focus on "equipment and working capital."

1) Organizations, links, and priority order

BDC — Business Development Bank of Canada

LoanVery High Priority

Best for: working capital, marketing, payroll, software development, and growth projects.

Target amount: $10,000 to $350,000; for us, an initial request of $30,000 to $50,000 is more realistic.

Note: BDC offers entrepreneur-focused loans, and the initial online request is commonly introduced as no-credit-check at that first step.

3) Acceptable proposal structure

The employee should create a tailored version for each program, but all proposals should follow this base structure:

Executive Summary

In 150-250 words: what Topping Courier is, what problem it solves, how much funding is requested, and what outcomes are expected.

Problem Statement

Market problem: SMEs in the GTA do not have a smart, transparent, and cost-efficient system for same-day/next-day B2B delivery.

Solution

Topping solution: an AI logistics platform including smart dispatch, route optimization, CRM, customer portal, and B2B reporting.

Innovation

Describe innovation technically: driver-allocation algorithm, recurring-order analysis, density planning, capacity forecasting, and per-route cost reduction.

Market & Customers

Target market: medical supplies distributors, e-commerce fulfillment, food distribution, wholesale, and recurring B2B delivery in the GTA.

Project Budget

For $30,000: $12k AI/CRM development, $8k route optimization and testing, $5k sales/market validation, $3k documentation/compliance, $2k cloud/tools.

Milestones

30 days: design and MVP. 60 days: customer testing. 90 days: onboard 5-10 pilot B2B clients. 180 days: commercial version.

Economic Impact

Outcomes: revenue growth, job creation, lower delivery costs, stronger Canadian technology, and support for GTA SMEs.

4) Required business plan structures

BDC / Loan Version

Focus on repayment ability, revenue, customer base, cash flow, and risk control.

  • Company Overview
  • Revenue Model
  • Current Customers
  • Sales Pipeline
  • 12-Month Cash Flow
  • Loan Use of Funds
  • Repayment Plan

IRAP / OCI Version

Focus on innovation, technical risk, roadmap, and technology commercialization.

  • Technical Challenge
  • AI Roadmap
  • R&D Milestones
  • Commercialization
  • Market Validation
  • IP / Data Strategy
  • Team Capability

Enterprise / Advisory Version

Concise and execution-focused, suitable for advisory meetings and program referrals.

  • One-page Summary
  • Business Need
  • Funding Target
  • Documents Available
  • Programs Requested
  • Next Steps

7) 90-day execution plan

Day 1 to 3

Complete Business Benefits Finder. Contact Enterprise Toronto. Start BDC online eligibility. Log all outcomes in the Tracker.

Day 4 to 7

Collect company documents, bank statements, invoices, resumes, website links, and financial data.

Week 2

Create two files: 1) Business Plan for Loan 2) Technology Proposal for Grant.

Week 3

Follow up on BDC application, Enterprise Toronto meeting, OCI contact, and IRAP guidance request.

Week 4 to 6

Finalize pitch deck, $30k budget, milestones, and cash-flow forecast. Run weekly follow-ups.

Day 60 to 90

Keep at least 3 active applications, 2 advisory meetings, 1 loan file, and 1 technology-support file in progress.
