Executive Summary

We do not just sell;
we negotiate the order-flow center

Many delivery companies leave growth to scattered outreach and traditional sales. Topping Courier does not choose that path. We first identify each market center - the point where orders, decisions, data, or distribution flow - then focus negotiation on that point.

The CEO leads market intelligence and strategy architecture: analyzes the market, discovers and scores industry centers, then assigns them based on negotiator specialization. Neima, Kianoush, and Tina are all negotiators; they differ by the center types they focus on.

The end result is not only more clients; it is a repeatable negotiation system that starts from center discovery, moves through CRM, leads to meetings and proposals, and ends with recurring contracts, geographic density, and healthy cash flow.

“Do not chase customers; find the center that creates them.”

Hamid Barzin — CEO, Topping Courier Inc.

Negotiation Model Based on Influence Centers

In this system, every member is a negotiator; each person focuses on their specialized market centers.

Operational Definition of a Center

A center is a market point where the highest flow of orders, decisions, data, money, or trust passes through. Topping’s goal is not to chase each small customer separately; it is to identify centers that generate multiple destinations and recurring orders from one point.
