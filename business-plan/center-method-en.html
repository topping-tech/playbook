Strategic Diagnostic Tool

Center Method

A strategic diagnostic tool to identify high-impact points in market, logistics, sales, and system design. This method is not about absolute certainty; it is about finding the point that creates the highest system-wide impact before consuming major effort.

01 / Operational Definition

Center means a leverage point, not necessarily a physical middle.

If we can focus on only one point, which point creates the greatest impact on the entire system?
Center←Node←Density←Cash Flow←Control←Scale
02 / Centers of Influence

Every system can have multiple leverage centers.

The Center Method is not designed to force one universal answer; it compares centers and selects the best starting point.

👥

Customer Center

The point where the customer decides or starts an order: website, app, sales rep, or marketplace.

🧠

Decision Center

The person or unit that approves contracts, budgets, and process changes: CEO, operations, or procurement.

📦

Logistics Center

The point where orders are consolidated and dispatched: warehouse, distribution center, or fulfillment hub.

🔌

Data Center

The system producing order data: Shopify, ERP, CRM, WooCommerce, or internal files.

Decision Criteria

This section prevents slogan-based thinking. Instead of saying "this center is important," we ask concrete questions: Does it have order volume? Does it repeat? Is the decision-maker accessible? Does it support software integration? Does it convert to cash quickly?


Suggested rule: Nodes scoring above 40 should be first-priority B2B sales targets. Scores between 30 and 40 need additional validation. Below 30 means high effort and likely not a primary Center.

04 / Topping Execution Example

Medical supplies market: traditional approach vs. Center approach

Traditional Approach

Contacting many clinics, pharmacies, or small stores one by one. This usually leads to high cost, slow progress, and fragmented orders.

Center Method Approach

First identify which warehouse, software, or operations manager the order flow passes through. If that node is acquired, dozens of destinations can enter Topping’s network indirectly.

05 / Execution Roadmap

From idea to real contract

Market Analysis

Select one specific industry; do not target all markets at once.

Node Identification

List customer, decision, logistics, and data centers.

Scoring

Use the scorecard to identify the best starting node.

Tailored Offer

Design an actionable, specific proposal for that exact center.

Small Test

Run a pilot with one real customer, one small route, and limited data.

Build Density

After validation, concentrate orders in a defined zone and route.
