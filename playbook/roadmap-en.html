2. Project Story

The project started from a simple need: business owners still use business cards, but traditional cards provide no data, leads, reporting, or tracking.

The initial idea was to provide NFC cards to Owners so they could introduce their professional digital profile. Then the idea evolved: if each tap also creates value for Sponsors, the NFC card becomes a distribution and sales channel.

As a result, VELOXA moved beyond an "NFC card" concept and became a marketing, lead-generation, and CRM infrastructure for a network of business owners.

Core statement: NFC is not the final product; NFC is the gateway to a lead-generation and business-connection network.

A business owner, realtor, lawyer, spa owner, mortgage broker, or any professional who receives a personal NFC card.

6. User Journeys

Owner Journey

  1. Owner is selected or invited.
  2. Receives a free NFC card or premium package.
  3. Completes profile setup.
  4. Introduces the card to customers and network.
  5. Receives tap and interaction analytics.
  6. Gradually upgrades to advanced services, CRM, or team packages.

Sponsor Journey

  1. Sponsor selects target city or industry.
  2. Teaser and offer campaign are built.
  3. When Owner cards are tapped, sponsor teaser appears.
  4. User clicks CTA.
  5. Lead form is submitted.
  6. Lead is saved in CRM and reported to Sponsor.

Audience/Customer Journey

  1. Taps NFC card.
  2. Sees sponsor teaser.
  3. If interested, claims the offer.
  4. Then views Owner profile.
  5. Calls, saves contact, or opens links.

Admin Journey

  1. Manages owners and cards.
  2. Defines sponsors.
  3. Configures placement and targeting.
  4. Views, filters, and exports leads.
  5. Creates monthly sponsor reports.

19. Direct message to developer

Please do not build this as an NFC Business Card App. That framing will derail the product strategy.

VELOXA's core product is a Business Owner Lead Network. NFC is only the entry point. Every technical decision should be evaluated with this question:

Does this feature improve lead generation, CRM management, owner network growth, or sponsor revenue?

If the answer is no, it is not a current priority.

Development priorities: Lead Engine, CRM, Sponsor Reports, Owner Activation, Analytics, Referral System.
